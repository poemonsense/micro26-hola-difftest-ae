\section{Artifact Appendix}

\subsection{Overview}

This artifact reproduces the HOLA results in Section 6.2: the RCache trade-off
data in Figures 11--13, the phased Linux-boot plot in Figure 14, and the RCore
Chisel line counts in Table 4. It includes the HOLA synthesizable reference
model (SVM), the NutShell RISC-V processor, a Linux boot image, and an automated
evaluation workflow. All hardware experiments use Verilator; no FPGA or
Palladium access is required.

\subsection{Artifact Metadata}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Program:} SVM synthesizable reference model and NutShell
        RISC-V processor.
  \item \textbf{Compilation:} Mill/Chisel elaboration, Verilator compilation,
        and RISC-V cross-compilation of the bootrom.
  \item \textbf{Transformations:} Chisel hardware descriptions to generated
        Verilog/SystemVerilog and Verilator executables.
  \item \textbf{Binary:} NutShell \path{ready-to-run/linux.bin} and generated
        configuration-specific simulator binaries.
  \item \textbf{Data set:} Linux boot image workload; no separate dataset.
  \item \textbf{Run-time environment:} OpenXiangShan xs-env Ubuntu 24.04
        Docker image with the provisioned toolchain.
  \item \textbf{Hardware:} 16-core x86-64 host with at least 32 GiB RAM;
        no accelerator or programmable logic is required.
  \item \textbf{Run-time state:} 31 manifest configurations: 30 expected-good
        runs and one expected cache-capacity abort.
  \item \textbf{Execution:} \path{./run-all.sh} builds the image, runs the
        workflow, and generates the evaluation reports.
  \item \textbf{Metrics:} Evict miss rate, instruction miss rate, refill
        overhead, phased boot miss rate, IPC, load/store IPC, and source lines.
  \item \textbf{Output:} Five Markdown reports and one Figure 14 PDF plot,
        with raw logs and counter provenance.
  \item \textbf{Experiments:} Figures 11--14 and Table 4.
  \item \textbf{How much disk space required (approximately)?:} Approximately
        20 GiB for Docker images, caches, build products, and raw logs.
  \item \textbf{How much time is needed to prepare workflow (approximately)?:}
        Approximately 20--40 minutes.
  \item \textbf{How much time is needed to complete experiments (approximately)?:}
        Approximately 2--4 hours on a 16-core host.
  \item \textbf{Publicly available?:} Yes; the artifact and pinned sources are
        available at
        \url{https://github.com/poemonsense/micro26-hola-difftest-ae}.
  \item \textbf{Code licenses (if publicly available)?:} Apache License 2.0
        for SVM and Mulan PSL v2 for NutShell and difftest; nested dependencies
        include their license files.
  \item \textbf{Data licenses (if publicly available)?:} No separate dataset;
        the included workload follows the source licenses.
  \item \textbf{Workflow automation framework used?:} Bash scripts and Docker.
  \item \textbf{Archived (provide DOI)?:} The archival release is available at

\subsection{Running the Artifact}

The host requires Git and Docker Engine with Linux-container support. Run the
following two commands in order.

\paragraph{\textbf{Command 1}: Clone and enter the artifact.}

\begin{verbatim}
git clone --recursive \
  https://github.com/poemonsense/micro26-hola-difftest-ae
cd micro26-hola-difftest-ae
\end{verbatim}

\paragraph{\textbf{Command 2}: Run the complete evaluation.}

\begin{verbatim}
./run-all.sh
\end{verbatim}

\path{run-all.sh} builds the Docker environment and starts the evaluation.
Inside the container, \path{artifact-evaluation.sh} performs four fail-fast
stages:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item Validate the environment and initialize the pinned sources.
  \item Build the Verilator simulators for all RCache configurations.
  \item Boot \path{NutShell/ready-to-run/linux.bin} on each simulator and
        capture firmware output and simulator-counter snapshots.
  \item Compute the metrics and generate the reports.
\end{enumerate}

A successful run creates:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \texttt{evaluation/figure11.md}
  \item \texttt{evaluation/figure12.md}
  \item \texttt{evaluation/figure13.md}
  \item \texttt{evaluation/figure14.md}
  \item \texttt{evaluation/figure14.pdf}
  \item \texttt{evaluation/table4.md}
\end{itemize}

The Markdown reports include measured values, paper values, raw-log provenance,
and the calculation for each measured point. Figures 11--13 use final hardware
counters printed by the firmware. Figure 14 uses periodic simulator-counter
snapshots.

\subsection{Expected Results}

\paragraph{Figure 11: evict miss rate.}
The metric is
\[
100\times\mathtt{cache\_evict\_miss}/\mathtt{cache\_evict}.
\]

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{cccc}
\hline
Cache & 4-way & 8-way & 16-way \\
\hline
256 KiB & X & 2.76\% & 3.42\% \\
512 KiB & 0.60\% & 0.41\% & 0.36\% \\
1 MiB   & 0.06\% & 0.01\% & 0.00\% \\
2 MiB   & 0.02\% & 0.00\% & 0.00\% \\
\hline
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

The X is the expected abort for the 256 KiB/4-way configuration, where every
block becomes dirty and non-replaceable. The supplementary 256 KiB/8-way PLRU
point is 0.53\%.

\paragraph{Figure 12: instruction miss rate.}
The metric is
\[
100\times\mathtt{core\_out\_miss}/\mathtt{core\_out}.
\]

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{ccccc}
\hline
Configuration & 256 KiB & 512 KiB & 1 MiB & 2 MiB \\
\hline
2-bank, 2-port & 18.32\% & 2.08\% & 0.20\% & 0.19\% \\
2-bank, 3-port & 18.18\% & 1.89\% & 0.02\% & 0.00\% \\
4-bank, 2-port & 18.27\% & 2.00\% & 0.12\% & 0.11\% \\
4-bank, 3-port & 18.18\% & 1.89\% & 0.01\% & 0.00\% \\
\hline
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\paragraph{Figure 13: refill-on-read-miss.}
The disabled and enabled rows use the Figure 12 miss-rate metric. Bandwidth
overhead is
\[
100\times
\frac{\mathtt{cache\_read\_cache\_miss}}
     {\mathtt{cache\_refill}+\mathtt{cache\_evict}}.
\]

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{ccccc}
\hline
Series & 256 KiB & 512 KiB & 1 MiB & 2 MiB \\
\hline
Disabled & 18.32\% & 2.08\% & 0.20\% & 0.19\% \\
Enabled  & 0.70\% & 0.26\% & 0.19\% & 0.19\% \\
Overhead & 2.70\% & 0.41\% & 0.00\% & 0.00\% \\
\hline
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\paragraph{Figure 14: phased Linux-boot behavior.}
The Figure 14 PDF contains the four paper series: 2-port miss rate, 3-port miss
rate, IPC, and load/store IPC. The plot covers approximately
$300\times2^{16}$ reference cycles and should show the large early 2-port miss
spikes removed by the third port.

Both runs use a 512 KiB, 8-way cache with refill-on-read-miss. The 2-port run
uses two banks; the 3-port run uses four banks. Curves are derived from the
RTL's 1,024-cycle simulator-counter snapshots.

\paragraph{Table 4: RCore Chisel lines.}
The expected counts for MMU, FETCH, AGU, LSU, ARITH, PRIV, TRAP, COMMIT, and
RCore are 253, 61, 52, 126, 87, 154, 49, 25, and 129 physical source lines,
respectively, totaling 936 lines.
